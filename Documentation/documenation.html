2. Abstract

This document describes the design of a mobile mental health application that aims to support individuals in managing their well-being. The application provides users with a variety of features to track their mood, access personalized content and recommendations, engage in self-care activities, and connect with in-house mental health professionals for consultations.

4. How to Use the App

The mental health app provides a user-friendly interface to support your well-being journey. Here's a basic overview of how you can navigate its functionalities:

  1. Daily Check-in and Tracking

    Start your day by opening the app and visiting the daily check-in section. You'll be able to input your current mood, energy levels, and any additional prompts about your thoughts or experiences. This data is stored securely and used to personalize your app experience.

  2. Accessing Personalized Recommendations

    Based on your daily check-ins and other data, the app suggests personalized content and activities. This might include articles, videos, or infographics on relevant mental health topics. You might also receive recommendations for self-care activities, such as meditation exercises or physical routines tailored to your needs.

  3. Engaging in Self-Care Activities

    The app offers a variety of self-care activities you can integrate into your daily routine. Explore guided meditations with various lengths and difficulty levels, practice breathing exercises for stress management, or access educational resources on coping mechanisms and healthy habits.

  4. Connecting with a Psychologist

    The application offers users the functionality to arrange a personalized session with a qualified psychologist at their convenience.

  5. Monitoring Progress

    Some app features might help you track your progress over time. This could include visualizing your mood patterns through charts based on daily check-ins, monitoring your progress in specific areas through reports, or setting goals and tracking achievements.
